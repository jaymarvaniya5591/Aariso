Chapter 01 ⁄ Engineering

Specifications that
demand to be felt.

Six layers of world-class engineering — optics, radar, AI, light — stacked behind a single sheet of two-way glass. Nothing extraneous. Nothing compromised.

01

Two-way mirror glass.

Optical-grade silvered glass tuned for 70⁄30 transmission. Invisible when dark. Photographic when lit from within.

02

CRI 95+ lighting.

Studio-grade LEDs with a colour rendering index above 95 — accurate enough for skin diagnostics, dermatology, and makeup.

03

Sony IMX678 optics.

4K starlight-grade sensor with back-illuminated pixels. Reads microtexture, hydration, pigmentation — in the low light of a real bathroom.

04

60 GHz mmWave radar.

Presence, depth and fall detection through steam, darkness, and doubt. Works when cameras can’t — because privacy and safety can’t be trade-offs.

05

10″ edge display.

A single bonded display strip at the base of the mirror. Skin data, weather, the day ahead — visible only when you need it.

06

NVIDIA Jetson Orin NX.

100 TOPS of on-device inference. Every model runs here. No frame of you ever leaves the room — architecturally, it can’t.

Chapter 02 ⁄ Capabilities

Six things
your mirror should do for you.

Not a dashboard. Not a smart home hub. Six capabilities that belong exactly where you already are — mid-shave, mid-routine, mid-morning — and nowhere else.

i

Skin diagnostics.

Texture, hydration, early pigmentation, pores. What changed overnight, what to do about it today, what’s trending across the month.

ii

Grooming assistance.

Live zoomed preview as you shave or shape. Object detection that knows when you’re holding a razor. Symmetry guide that corrects by millimetres.

iii

AR makeup try-on.

Try shades in real, photographic light. Colour-tuned for Indian skin tones from the ground up — what you see is what buys.

iv

Fall detection + voice SOS.

mmWave radar detects a fall even through steam. Voice SOS reaches hotel PBX or your emergency contacts — seconds from the floor to a responder.

v

Circadian lighting.

The mirror reads the time, the room, and your presence. Morning: cool and bright for accurate skin reads. Night: warm and dim, automatically.

vi

Ambient intelligence.

Weather, your day, a personal briefing, ambient audio. The small things you used to reach for your phone for — now on the surface you’re already looking at.
